Funnel Analysis

Diagnosed a post-launch navigation failure in the product homepage by building a full browsing funnel tracking scroll and click rates at each section — surfacing three critical drop points (A→B, B→C, C→D) and identifying that users who stopped scrolling were being redirected to promotions instead of products. Findings were adopted by the product team, who restructured the homepage architecture to surface promotions earlier and embed products directly in categories.

Segmentation

Segmented the user base by product brand concentration (single/dual/multiple) to quantify loyalty patterns and uncover substitution behavior — finding that over 70% of users engaged with only 1–2 brands, and that among users avoiding mainstream brands, over 80% concentrated on a single alternative. Findings directly informed brand partnership prioritization and personalized recommendation strategy, with targeted promotions by brand preference profile recommended.

Churn Analysis

Investigated a significant active user decline across Market A and Market B by cross-analyzing login timing, credit balance structure, and agent activity status — identifying two distinct churn profiles ("has balance, not engaging" and "wants to engage, no balance") and surfacing suspicious agent inactivity in specific audit groups during a concentrated time window. Delivered differentiated re-engagement strategies per churn profile, with the anomalous agent groups flagged for further investigation.

ROI Analysis

Evaluated a seasonal campaign's business impact by segmenting conversion rates across user types (new/repeat/return) and brands — calculating an overall ROI of 4.54 and finding that 20% of users returned post-event, with an average return timing around Day 5–6 and average participation spanning 3+ days. Recommended Day 5–6 re-engagement notifications and extra rewards for users participating 4+ days; recommendations were adopted into the subsequent marketing strategy.
